# Member A – Engineering Analysis

## Role
As Member A, my responsibility, as outlined in the member assignments, was to translate machine learning results into civil engineering insights. The goal was to interpret climate outliers and determine how future conditions would impact infrastructure systems.

## Approach
I compared historical climate data with a synthetic 2070 dataset. I calculated differences across key climate indicators such as temperature, rainfall, and sea level rise. These differences were analyzed to identify engineering stressors.

## Key Indicators Analyzed
- Average temperature
- Heatwave days
- Rainfall change
- Sea level rise

## Engineering Interpretation

### HVAC Systems
Increased temperature and heatwave frequency lead to higher cooling demand. HVAC systems designed using historical data may not be sufficient for future conditions, resulting in increased energy usage and reduced efficiency.

### Structural Impact
Higher temperatures can cause thermal expansion in materials such as steel and concrete. Over time, this can lead to material fatigue and structural degradation.

### Flooding Risk
Increased rainfall and sea level rise increase the likelihood of flooding. This impacts drainage systems, foundations, and coastal infrastructure.

### Air Quality
Declining air quality indicates the need for improved ventilation and filtration systems in building design.

## Conclusion
The analysis shows that future infrastructure must be designed using predictive climate models rather than historical averages. Climate change introduces new risks that must be considered in engineering design to ensure long-term resilience.
